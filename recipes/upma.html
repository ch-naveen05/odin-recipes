Upma Recipe

Description

Upma is a popular South Indian breakfast dish made from semolina (also known as rava or suji). It is a savory porridge-like dish that is quick to prepare and can be customized with various vegetables and spices. Upma is known for its soft, fluffy texture and is often enjoyed with coconut chutney or sambar. The dish is typically cooked by roasting the semolina in ghee or oil, then adding water and cooking it until it reaches a thick, creamy consistency. Common ingredients include mustard seeds, curry leaves, green chilies, ginger, and various vegetables like onions, carrots, peas, and beans. Upma is a nutritious and filling meal, making it a favorite choice for breakfast or a light snack.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup semolina (rava/suji)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (optional)
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped vegetables (carrots, peas, beans) - optional
  • Coriander leaves for garnish
  • Juice of half a lemon (optional)

Preparation Steps

  1. Dry roast the semolina in a pan until it turns light golden. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add urad dal and chana dal (if using) and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, and curry leaves. Sauté until onions are translucent.
  5. If using vegetables, add them now and cook for a few minutes until they are slightly tender.
  6. Add water and salt to the pan. Bring it to a boil.
  7. Gradually add the roasted semolina while continuously stirring to avoid lumps.
  8. Cook on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the semolina absorbs the water and reaches a thick consistency.
  9. Turn off the heat. If desired, add lemon juice and mix well.
  10. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.
