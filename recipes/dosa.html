Dosa Recipe

Description

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter. It is a thin, crispy pancake that is typically served for breakfast or as a snack. Dosas are known for their light and airy texture, with a slightly tangy flavor due to the fermentation process. They can be plain or stuffed with various fillings such as spiced potatoes (masala dosa), paneer, or vegetables. Dosas are often accompanied by chutneys (coconut, tomato, mint) and sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew). The preparation involves soaking rice and urad dal, grinding them into a smooth batter, and allowing it to ferment overnight. The batter is then spread thinly on a hot griddle to create the dosa. Dosas are not only delicious but also nutritious, making them a favorite choice for many.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Steps

  1. Wash and soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind urad dal and fenugreek seeds to a smooth batter. Grind rice to a slightly coarse batter.
  3. Mix both batters together, add salt, and let it ferment overnight or for 8-12 hours.
  4. Heat a non-stick griddle or tawa over medium heat.
  5. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the griddle and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin pancake.
  6. Drizzle some oil around the edges and cook until golden brown and crispy.
  7. Flip and cook the other side for a minute (optional).
  8. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.
