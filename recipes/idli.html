Idli Recipe

Description

Idli is a classic South Indian breakfast dish made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). The batter is steamed in special molds to create soft, fluffy, and mildly tangy rice cakes that are light and easy to digest. Idlis are especially popular for breakfast across Southern India and are traditionally served hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
The preparation involves soaking rice and lentils separately, grinding them to a smooth paste, and allowing the mixture to ferment overnight before steaming. This process produces a naturally leavened, protein-rich, and nutritious food, enjoyed by people of all ages and also considered an ideal choice for a healthy meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Preparation Steps

  1. Wash and soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind urad dal and fenugreek seeds to a smooth batter. Grind rice to a slightly coarse batter.
  3. Mix both batters together, add salt, and let it ferment overnight or for 8-12 hours.
  4. Grease idli molds and pour the batter into them.
  5. Steam for 10-12 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.
